Country Overview

Explore Tunisia

Tunisia, a captivating North African destination rich in history, culture, and natural beauty. This remarkable country boasts a myriad of fascinating facts and attractions that will pique your curiosity and ignite your wanderlust.

  • Historical Marvels: Tunisia is home to the ancient city of Carthage, founded by the Phoenicians in the 9th century BC. It played a pivotal role in the history of the Mediterranean and was eventually destroyed by the Romans in 146 BC. Today, you can wander through its ruins and discover this fascinating history firsthand.
  • Roman Legacy: : The El Djem amphitheater, often referred to as the "Colosseum of North Africa," is one of the world's best-preserved Roman amphitheaters. It's a stunning testament to the grandeur of the Roman Empire and is a UNESCO World Heritage site.
  • Medina of Tunis: The Medina of Tunis, a UNESCO World Heritage site, is a labyrinth of narrow streets, historic palaces, and vibrant markets. It's a fantastic place to immerse yourself in local culture and shop for unique handicrafts and textiles.
  • Sahara Desert: Tunisia offers a gateway to the Sahara Desert, which covers a significant portion of the country. Its mesmerizing sand dunes and hidden oases create a surreal landscape that's perfect for desert adventures, including camel treks and stargazing.
  • Ksar Ouled Soltane: This stunning granary, located in Tataouine, showcases traditional Berber architecture. It's a remarkable example of how ancient design and construction techniques were used to store grains in a harsh desert environment.
  • Diverse Landscapes: Tunisia's geography ranges from Mediterranean beaches to arid deserts, fertile plains, and lush oases. The country's diverse landscapes offer something for every traveler, from water sports on the coast to hiking in the Atlas Mountains.
  • Warm Hospitality: Tunisians are known for their warm and welcoming nature. You'll find the local people to be friendly and eager to share their culture, making your journey all the more memorable.

Tunisia's unique blend of history, culture, and natural wonders make it a top travel destination. Whether you're a history buff, an adventurer, or simply seeking a taste of North African charm, Tunisia has something extraordinary to offer every traveler.

Tunisian National Events

Aoussou

Aoussou is a lively Tunisian festival that showcases the country's vibrant culture through music, dance, storytelling, and traditional crafts

Carthage Festival

The Carthage Festival is a renowned cultural event held at the historic Carthage amphitheater, featuring music, dance, theater, and visual arts from around the world.

Mawlid nabawi in Kairouan

Mawlid nabawi is a joyous celebration of the Prophet Muhammad's birthday, marked by prayers and communal meals, symbolizing unity and the coexistence of Islamic traditions and Tunisian culture.
